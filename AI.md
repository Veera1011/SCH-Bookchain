Since we've already set up the foundation with the AI Assistant and Groq/Gemini integration, we can take the "BookChain" experience to the next level. 

Here are several AI-powered features that would feel modern and highly useful:

### For Readers (Employees)
1.  **AI Reading Companion**:
    *   **Book "TL;DR"**: Generate 3-5 bullet point takeaways or a "1-minute summary" for any book to help users decide if they want to borrow it.
    *   **Discussion Starter**: Generate 3 high-quality discussion questions for the user to think about after they finish reading.
2.  **Semantic "Mood" Search**: 
    *   Instead of just title searches, allow users to type: *"I want something that feels like 'Deep Work' but for creative people"* or *"I'm feeling burnt out and need inspiration."*
3.  **Visual ISBN/Cover Scanner**: 
    *   Allow users to point their camera at any physical book (even if it's not in the library yet) to see if the library has it, or to see "AI reviews" and ratings instantly.
4.  **Personalized Reading Pathways**: 
    *   An AI agent that looks at their borrow history and creates a "Learning Path" (e.g., *"Based on your interest in Leadership, here are the next 3 books you should read to master the skill"*).

### For Librarians (Admins)
1.  **Smart Categorization**:
    *   Automatically generate descriptive "Micro-Tags" for books based on their descriptions (e.g., #Focus, #Resilience, #PythonBasics) to make browsing more granular.
2.  **Demand Forecasting**:
    *   AI that analyzes borrow trends and notifies the admin: *"Title X is trending; you might need 2 more copies to keep up with demand."*
3.  **Automated Inventory Ingestion**:
    *   Upload a photo of a shelf, and AI identifies all books present, checking them against the digital database to find missing or misplaced items.

### Which path sounds most interesting?
*   **The "Summarizer/TL;DR"** feature is usually the biggest hit for busy employees.
*   **The "Mood Search"** makes the app feel very high-tech and "intelligent."
